Body Temperature

Normal body temperature for adults is about 98.6°F (37°C).

When to contact a doctor:

  • If your temperature is higher than 100.4°F (38°C) for more than 24 hours.
  • If your temperature is lower than 95°F (35°C).

Normal range: 97°F to 99°F (36.1°C to 37.2°C).

Source: CDC

Blood Pressure

Systolic pressure is the pressure when the heart beats, while diastolic pressure is the pressure when the heart rests between beats.

Normal blood pressure for adults is:

  • Systolic pressure: less than 120 mmHg
  • Diastolic pressure: less than 80 mmHg

When to contact a doctor:

  • If your blood pressure is consistently higher than 140/90 mmHg.
  • If you experience symptoms such as headache, shortness of breath, chest pain, or blurred vision.

Normal range: Systolic pressure less than 120 mmHg, Diastolic pressure less than 80 mmHg.

Source: American Heart Association

Heart Rate

Normal resting heart rate for adults is between 60 and 100 beats per minute.

When to contact a doctor:

  • If your resting heart rate is consistently above 100 beats per minute.
  • If you experience symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or fainting.

Normal range: 60 to 100 beats per minute.

Source: Mayo Clinic
